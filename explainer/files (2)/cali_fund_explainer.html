Cali Fund Allocation Model

Inverted UN Scale of Assessment Option

An interactive guide to the five key concepts behind the model. For exploratory and discussion purposes — all figures are illustrative.

Purpose

The Cali Fund distributes biodiversity finance among countries that are Parties to the Convention on Biological Diversity (CBD). This model produces indicative, illustrative shares — not binding entitlements — to support negotiations.

Core principle
Countries that contribute less to the UN budget receive proportionally more from this fund
Special protections
Least Developed Countries, Small Island Developing States, and Indigenous Peoples & Local Communities all receive targeted support
The model uses the UN Scale of Assessments 2025–2027 as its input — a widely-accepted measure of countries' relative economic capacity.

What the controls do

Fund size  Sets the total annual pot in USD   Land area weight  Adjusts how much land stewardship matters   Island states weight  Reserves a pool for small island nations   IPLC share  Splits each allocation between governments and IPLCs

The Formula

The model has one foundation and two adjustments that together determine every country's share.

Each country's share =
  Inverted UN Scale of Assessment Foundation (IUSAF) — the base
  + Terrestrial Stewardship Allocation Component (TSAC) — adjustment for large countries
  + SIDS Ocean Stewardship Allocation Component (SOSAC) — adjustment for island states
IUSAF · Foundation
The core equity engine. Inverts UN budget shares so smaller contributors receive a larger fund share. All allocations start here.
TSAC · Adjustment
Recognises the terrestrial biodiversity stewardship responsibilities of countries with large land areas.
SOSAC · Adjustment
Recognises the ocean stewardship responsibilities and structural constraints of Small Island Developing States.
IUSAF is the foundation. TSAC and SOSAC are adjustments — any weight given to them reallocates money away from the IUSAF base. It is a matter for Parties to agree the appropriate balance.

Establishing Equity (IUSAF)

The UN Scale says how much each country contributes to the UN budget. The model flips this: the smaller your UN share, the larger your fund share. This is the foundation of the model.

Fund share = 1 ÷ UN budget share  →  then rescaled so all shares add up to 100%

Recognising Stewardship (TSAC & SOSAC)

Large countries: terrestrial biodiversity stewardship (TSAC)

Some countries are very large and therefore carry greater responsibility for stewarding the world's terrestrial biodiversity. A land-area weight recognises this stewardship role and adjusts their share accordingly.

Small Island Developing States: ocean stewardship and structural constraints (SOSAC)

Small Island Developing States carry significant ocean stewardship responsibilities and face recognised structural constraints — geographic isolation, economic vulnerability, and disproportionate exposure to environmental risks. An equal share among eligible SIDS balances these realities.

The three components are weighted so they always add up to 100% of the fund. Giving more weight to land area (TSAC) to recognise the biodiversity stewardship responsibilities of larger countries or the ocean stewardship responsibilities of SIDS (SOSAC) reallocates money away from the inverted UN scale (IUSAF). It would be a matter for Parties to agree the appropriate balance points between IUSAF, TSAC and SOSAC.

Any weight given to TSAC or SOSAC reallocates from the IUSAF foundation. The three pools always sum to 100% of the fund.

Recognising IPLCs

For simplicity the model is organised around countries as Parties. In accordance with Decision 16/2 at least 50% of Cali Funding will be allocated to IPLCs. This is achieved by splitting the allocation in half.

It is important to recognise that the amount allocated to IPLCs by the formula and appropriate pathways to disbursement are separate considerations. Different pathways to disbursement may be appropriate at the national, subnational or UN regional/sub-regional level and may be informed by existing experience, guiding principles and the programme of work of the Subsidiary Body on Article 8J and Other Provisions of the Convention.
IPLC share = total allocation × your chosen %
