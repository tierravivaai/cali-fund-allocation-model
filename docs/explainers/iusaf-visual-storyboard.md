# IUSAF Visual Storyboard

Purpose: shared visual treatment for short explainers, slides, and 1–2 minute introductory videos.

## Unifying metaphor

Use a **fairness watering can** or **flipped scale**.

- The **watering can** works best for young audiences.
- The **flipped scale** works best for older children and adults.
- Both metaphors communicate the same idea: a known measure of economic capacity is turned into a fair allocation system.

## Key visual grammar

| Concept | Visual | Colour |
|---|---|---|
| Cali Fund | Watering can, global fund, or shared pool | Gold |
| Inverted UN Scale / economic fairness | Flipped scale, ladder, or main stream of water | Gold or warm yellow |
| Terrestrial stewardship | Land, trees, forests, map area, green overlay | Green |
| Ocean stewardship for SIDS | Islands, waves, ocean area, blue overlay | Blue |
| Indigenous Peoples and local communities | People, roots, community circle, second channel | Purple or earth tone |
| State component | Public building, government channel, first basket | Neutral grey |

## One-minute video structure

| Time | Scene | Visual direction | Core message |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 0–10s | The question | Map, nature icons, shared fund | “How should the Cali Fund be shared fairly?” |
| 10–25s | The base | UN contribution scale flips | “Use the UN Scale, but invert it so lower economic capacity means more support.” |
| 25–40s | The bands | Six steps or bands | “Group countries into bands to keep the model simple and transparent.” |
| 40–55s | Stewardship | Small green and blue overlays | “Add modest recognition for land and ocean stewardship.” |
| 55–70s | IPLC split | Allocation divides into State and IPLC channels | “At least half supports Indigenous Peoples and local communities.” |
| 70–85s | The formula | Simple formula appears | “Country share = economic fairness + land stewardship + ocean stewardship.” |
| 85–100s | Closing image | Healthy garden or living map | “Fairness plus stewardship, with communities built in.” |

## Six-slide version

### Slide 1: The problem

**Title:** How should the Cali Fund be shared fairly?

**Visual:** World map with forests, islands, oceans, and countries of different economic capacity.

**Speaker note:** The Cali Fund needs a transparent allocation formula for biodiversity funding.

### Slide 2: Start with a known scale

**Title:** Start with the UN Scale of Assessments

**Visual:** Contribution ladder showing higher-capacity countries paying more into the UN.

**Speaker note:** The UN Scale is already used to assess economic capacity for UN budget contributions.

### Slide 3: Turn it around

**Title:** Flip the scale

**Visual:** The contribution ladder flips into an allocation ladder.

**Speaker note:** The proposal inverts the scale so lower assessed economic capacity receives larger support.

### Slide 4: Add stewardship

**Title:** Recognise land and ocean responsibilities

**Visual:** Large IUSAF base with small green TSAC and blue SOSAC overlays.

**Speaker note:** The stewardship components are small adjustments, not replacements for the equity base.

### Slide 5: Build in communities

**Title:** At least half for Indigenous Peoples and local communities

**Visual:** Each country allocation splits into two channels: State and IPLC.

**Speaker note:** IPLCs are integral beneficiaries of the allocation model.

### Slide 6: The simple formula

**Title:** Fairness plus stewardship

**Visual:** `Country Share = Inverted UN Scale + Land Stewardship + Ocean Stewardship`

**Speaker note:** The model combines economic fairness, biodiversity stewardship, and direct recognition of Indigenous Peoples and local communities.

## Short tagline options

1. “Flip the scale. Fund fairness. Support stewardship.”
2. “Economic fairness, land and ocean stewardship, communities built in.”
3. “A simple way to share the Cali Fund fairly.”
4. “From ability to pay to need for support.”

## Visual production notes

- Keep formulas out of the four-year-old version.
- Introduce acronyms only in the adult version or technical slides.
- Show TSAC and SOSAC as visibly smaller than the IUSAF base.
- Show IPLC support as structural, not optional: use a split channel or root system.
- Avoid implying that land area or SIDS status are perfect measures; describe them as simple proxies or recognitions.
