# The Earth Garden

Audience: around 4 years old  
Format: 1 minute voiceover, picture book, simple slide, or short animation

## Core idea

The Cali Fund is like a big watering can for the Earth garden. It shares help fairly, so places that need more help can care for animals, plants, forests, and oceans.

## Visual metaphor

A big garden has many plants. Some plants are small, some are tall, some grow on islands, and some grow beside forests. A watering can labelled “Cali Fund” shares water with them.

## Script

This is the Earth garden.

Every plant needs water to grow.

Some plants are big. Some plants are small. Some plants live on islands. Some plants live near forests.

The Cali Fund is like a big watering can.

It shares water in a fair way.

Places with less money get more water, because they need more help to care for nature.

Some places care for big forests and land. They get a little green drop.

Some island places care for big oceans. They get a little blue drop.

The water is also shared with Indigenous Peoples and local communities, because they help look after nature too.

When the water is shared fairly, the whole Earth garden can grow.

## Slide or scene plan

| Scene | Visual | Narration |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Earth as a garden with many plants | “This is the Earth garden.” |
| 2 | A large watering can appears | “The Cali Fund is like a big watering can.” |
| 3 | Smaller or drier plants receive more water | “It shares water in a fair way.” |
| 4 | Green drop falls on forests and land | “Some places care for big forests and land.” |
| 5 | Blue drop falls on islands and oceans | “Some island places care for big oceans.” |
| 6 | Each plant has two roots: a building and people holding hands | “The water is shared with governments and Indigenous Peoples and local communities.” |
| 7 | Flowers, birds, fish, and trees appear | “When we share fairly, the whole Earth garden can grow.” |

## Suggested visual style

- Soft colours.
- Friendly shapes.
- No numbers or formulas.
- Use water, plants, roots, islands, fish, birds, and trees.
