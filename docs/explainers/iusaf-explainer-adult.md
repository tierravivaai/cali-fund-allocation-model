# A Simple Formula for Fair Biodiversity Funding

Audience: intelligent adult without prior knowledge  
Format: 1–2 minute video introduction, opening presentation slide sequence, or briefing explainer

## Core idea

The Inverted UN Scale of Assessments Formula, or IUSAF, is a proposed way to allocate Cali Fund resources. It combines economic fairness with biodiversity stewardship, while treating Indigenous Peoples and local communities as integral beneficiaries.

## Script

The Cali Fund needs a transparent way to share money for biodiversity.

The challenge is that countries differ in two important ways.

First, they differ in economic capacity. Some countries have far fewer resources to protect and use biodiversity sustainably.

Second, they differ in stewardship responsibilities. Some countries care for very large land areas. Small Island Developing States often have responsibility for very large ocean spaces.

This proposal begins with the United Nations Scale of Assessments. That scale is normally used to decide how much countries contribute to the UN budget. It reflects economic capacity.

The formula turns that scale around.

Instead of using the scale to ask how much a country should pay, it uses the scale to ask how much support a country should receive.

Countries with lower assessed economic capacity receive larger shares. This inverted scale is the foundation of the model.

Two modest stewardship components are then added.

The terrestrial component recognises countries with large land areas. The ocean component recognises Small Island Developing States and their ocean stewardship responsibilities.

These components are deliberately small overlays. They do not replace the economic fairness base. They adjust it.

The model also builds in support for Indigenous Peoples and local communities. At least half of each geographic allocation is directed to them, recognising their central role in conservation and sustainable use.

In plain English, the formula says:

Share the fund according to economic capacity, add limited recognition for land and ocean stewardship, and make sure Indigenous Peoples and local communities receive at least half.

## Plain-language formula

```text
Country Share = Inverted UN Scale + Terrestrial Stewardship + Ocean Stewardship
```

## Technical shorthand

```text
Country Share = IUSAF + TSAC + SOSAC
```

Where:

- `IUSAF` is the inverted UN Scale base.
- `TSAC` is the terrestrial stewardship adjustment.
- `SOSAC` is the Small Island Developing States ocean stewardship adjustment.

## Slide or scene plan

| Scene | Visual | Narration |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | World map with forests, islands, oceans, and unequal economic capacity | “The Cali Fund needs a transparent way to share money for biodiversity.” |
| 2 | Two icons: wallet and biodiversity landscape | “The challenge is to recognise economic capacity and stewardship responsibility.” |
| 3 | UN contribution ladder | “The proposal begins with the United Nations Scale of Assessments.” |
| 4 | The ladder flips into an allocation ladder | “The formula turns that scale around.” |
| 5 | Six descending bands | “Countries with lower assessed economic capacity receive larger shares.” |
| 6 | Large base bar labelled “IUSAF” | “This inverted scale is the foundation.” |
| 7 | Small green overlay labelled “TSAC” | “A terrestrial component recognises large land stewardship responsibilities.” |
| 8 | Small blue overlay labelled “SOSAC” | “An ocean component recognises Small Island Developing States.” |
| 9 | Each allocation splits into two equal channels | “At least half supports Indigenous Peoples and local communities.” |
| 10 | Final formula | “Economic fairness, plus stewardship, with IPLCs built in.” |

## Suggested visual style

- Use one dominant image: a scale turning into a distribution ladder.
- Keep the IUSAF base visually much larger than the TSAC and SOSAC overlays.
- Show the IPLC commitment as a repeated 50/50 split, not as a footnote.
- Use muted policy colours with clear accent colours: green for land, blue for ocean, gold for fairness.
