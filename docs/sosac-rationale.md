# Rationale for SOSAC Parameter Setting (γ = 3%)

## Summary

The SOSAC weight of 3% (γ = 0.03) is the minimum positive integer percentage at which the SIDS Ocean Stewardship Allocation Component constitutes a double-digit share of the IUSAF baseline allocation for SIDS Parties in Bands 2 and above. At lower integer settings (1% or 2%), the SOSAC increment remains below 10% of the IUSAF component for all SIDS bands, which is arguably insufficient to constitute a meaningful recognition of the distinct stewardship responsibilities identified in COP Decision 16/2.

## Treaty Basis

COP Decision 16/2 (Annex, paras 1 and 18) establishes that the Cali Fund shall allocate resources in a "fair, equitable, transparent, accountable and gender-responsive manner" and, in particular, that:

> "Funding should support the realization of the objectives of the Convention in developing country Parties, **in particular the least developed countries and small island developing States**."

The phrase "in particular" creates a differential obligation toward SIDS beyond the general commitment to developing country Parties. SOSAC operationalises this obligation by directing a configurable weight toward all eligible SIDS on an equal-share basis.

## Design Logic

SOSAC allocates its weight equally among eligible SIDS Parties (1/n where n = number of eligible SIDS), rather than proportionally to marine area or EEZ size. This design choice was made on three grounds:

1. **Simplicity and transparency**: Equal shares avoid the contestable calculations and data-quality issues inherent in EEZ or marine-area measurement.
2. **Equity among SIDS**: An equal-share approach prevents larger archipelagic SIDS from capturing disproportionate SOSAC weight at the expense of smaller island states — consistent with the model's equity foundations.
3. **Policy legibility**: Negotiators can understand and verify the SOSAC allocation without specialised geographic data.

## Why 3% and Not 1% or 2%

The fund size is USD 1,000 M and there are 39 eligible SIDS. At each integer percentage:

| γ | SOSAC Pool (USD M) | Per SIDS (USD M) | SOSAC as % of IUSAF (Band 1) | SOSAC as % of IUSAF (Band 2+) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1% | 10.0 | 0.256 | 3.0% | 3.5–4.7% |
| 2% | 20.0 | 0.513 | 6.0% | 6.9–9.5% |
| **3%** | **30.0** | **0.769** | **9.0%** | **10.4–14.2%** |
| 4% | 40.0 | 1.026 | 12.0% | 13.9–19.0% |
| 5% | 50.0 | 1.282 | 15.0% | 17.4–23.7% |

At γ = 1% and γ = 2%, the SOSAC component represents less than 10% of the IUSAF baseline for every SIDS band. The uplift is present but arguably too modest to constitute a substantive recognition of the "in particular" mandate.

**At γ = 3%, SOSAC first reaches a double-digit share of IUSAF (~10%) for SIDS Parties in Bands 2 and above, and approaches 10% even for Band 1 SIDS (9.0%).** This is the minimum integer threshold at which the SOSAC uplift can be characterised as a meaningful — while still clearly modest and non-dominant — recognition of SIDS stewardship responsibilities.

## Constraint Verification

At γ = 3%, SOSAC remains well within the model's structural constraints:

- **IUSAF dominance**: With TSAC = 5% and SOSAC = 3%, the combined stewardship weight is 8%. IUSAF contributes 92% of allocation weight — firmly in the "equity-driven" space.
- **Far from overturn**: The SOSAC analytical balance point (where SOSAC overtakes IUSAF for SIDS) is approximately γ = 17.4%. The current setting is 5.8× below this threshold.
- **Far from model overturn**: The combined stewardship overturn threshold is TSAC + SOSAC = 50%. At 8%, the current position is 42 percentage points below model overturn.
- **Rank preservation**: At γ = 3% alone (without TSAC), no SIDS Party changes rank relative to pure IUSAF. The equal-share mechanism preserves rank order among SIDS.

## Characterisation

γ = 3% is best characterised as the **minimum integer percentage that provides a substantively meaningful SIDS uplift** while preserving IUSAF as the dominant allocation base. It is not analytically "optimal" in any formal sense — it does not minimise Gini, maximise Spearman, or satisfy a balance-point condition. Its grounding is:

1. The COP 16/2 "in particular" mandate for SIDS;
2. The double-digit SOSAC/IUSAF threshold as the minimum integer for substantive recognition;
3. The preservation of IUSAF dominance and structural integrity of the model.

## What Would Change at Higher Settings

- γ = 4–5%: SOSAC/IUSAF reaches 12–24% for Band 2+ SIDS. Still modest but increasingly visible. Would require separate justification beyond the "minimum meaningful" argument.
- γ ≈ 12.5%: TSAC overturn threshold (if TSAC were set at its crossover point).
- γ ≈ 17.4%: SOSAC analytical balance point — SOSAC overtakes IUSAF for the most affected SIDS.
- γ + β > 50%: Model overturn — stewardship becomes the dominant allocation driver.
